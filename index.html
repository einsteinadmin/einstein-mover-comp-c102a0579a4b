📖 How to use this tool

What this is

A live scenario modeler for mover pay. Plug in different combinations of base pay and variable pay per position, and see the company-wide cost, the gross-margin impact, and what the announcement looks like to an individual mover. The point isn't to predict the future — it's to make trade-offs visible before we commit to anything.

Three scenarios, one baseline. Everything is compared against Current (locked, not editable). Scenarios A, B, and C are sandboxes you can edit. Switch between them with the tabs above the position matrix.

The three pre-loaded scenarios

Scenario A — "Flat $20 Co-Pilot, raises across leads." Co-Pilot base jumps to $20/hr but loses its variable component (Cameron's hypothesis: simpler entry-level pay attracts more applicants and removes a confusing piece of the offer). All lead positions get base + variable raises. Net impact ≈ +$558K / +6% vs current.
Scenario B — "$20 Co-Pilot keeping the variable." Same as A, but Co-Pilots also keep their $1.50/hr variable. Tests Paul's hypothesis that variable still matters at entry-level. Lead raises identical to A. Net impact ≈ +$850K / +9% vs current.
Scenario C — "No-change baseline." A copy of Current, editable. Useful when you want to test ONE specific change in isolation without rebuilding from scratch.

The position matrix — what each column means

  • Headcount — total people in that position company-wide.
  • Diff HC — how many of that headcount sit at Houston + San Antonio (see "Branch differential" below).
  • Hrs/yr — blended average hours per year per person at that position. Should reflect your real FT/PT mix across the full year (summer is heavier, winter lighter — average it).
  • Base $/hr — what standard branches pay as base. Houston + SAA pay this minus the differential.
  • Var Max $/hr — the maximum quarterly variable a high performer can earn (same at every branch).
  • TCC Low / Med / High — Total Cash Comp per hour at each performance tier (base + earned variable).
  • Raise % L / M / H — how much each tier moves vs Current at the same performance level. Apples-to-apples.

Branch differential (Houston + San Antonio)

Houston and San Antonio pay base $1/hr below the other 8 branches across every position. The variable component is identical company-wide. The "Branch Differential $/hr" global lets you change that delta — set it to 0 to model a unified pay band, or test -0.50 to see what closing half the gap costs.

The Diff HC column tells the math how many of each position's headcount are at the differential branches. Defaults seed at 20% of each position (rough estimate — 2 of 10 branches). The "Apply % to all positions" button lets you bulk-fill all rows at once if you want a different starting estimate.

Why this matters: a $1/hr base raise that "feels" cheap costs roughly $90K/year just for the ~50 movers at Houston + SAA, on top of the standard-branch cost. Modeling the two cohorts separately keeps the total honest.

Performance distribution & multipliers

The annual cost calc assumes most movers earn the middle tier of variable, not the max. You tell the model two things:

  • Distribution — what % of movers we expect to be Low / Medium / High performers. Default 20 / 60 / 20.
  • Variable multipliers — what % of the max variable each tier actually earns. Default 50% / 80% / 100%.

So a position with $2.00/hr max variable, under defaults, costs roughly 0.2×$1.00 + 0.6×$1.60 + 0.2×$2.00 = $1.56/hr in expected variable on average. Adjust to match what your actuals show.

FTE basis & Implied FTEs

Raw headcount doesn't tell you what the workload actually is — a part-time mover working 800 hrs/yr counts as "1 head" but is really 0.4 of a full-time-equivalent (at a 2,080 hr basis).

The "Implied FTEs" card divides total mover-hours by the FTE basis (default 2,080 = 40 hrs × 52 wks). If the number is meaningfully below raw headcount, you've got a heavy PT mix and the per-position Hrs/yr inputs should reflect that blended average.

This does not affect cost math. Cost is driven by Headcount × Hrs/yr × $/hr. The Implied FTE card is a sanity-check so you can spot when Hrs/yr defaults are too high (or too low) for a position.

Mover-view preview

The bottom section shows what an individual mover at a given position sees when we announce the new program. Pick the position, scenario, and branch tier (Standard vs. Houston/SAA). The annual TCC row is calibrated to that position's Hrs/yr — so it's a realistic year-one earnings projection at low / medium / high performance.

Use this to sanity-check that the announcement actually feels like a raise to the mover you're talking to. If the high-performer line moves but the low-performer line is flat, that's worth knowing before you ship.

Aggregate impact cards (top right of the Aggregate section)

  • Total Annual Mover Comp — the company-wide annual cost for the active scenario.
  • Mover Comp ÷ Revenue — current run-rate is ~38–41%. Industry typical 30–40%. The cleanest mover-only affordability metric.
  • Total Comp ÷ AGM — only useful if you fill in "All Other Annual Comp" (managers + ops + leadership). Compared against the ~16% ceiling from the philosophy doc.
  • Blended Mover $/hr — total cost ÷ total mover-hours. The "average mover" wage.
  • Implied FTEs — see above.

Sharing & saving

Use the 📎 Copy share link button (bottom of page). It packs your full state — all 3 scenarios, every input — into the URL and copies it to your clipboard. Paste in Slack or email and the recipient sees exactly what you see. State also auto-saves to your browser; close the tab and your work comes back.

One thing this tool does NOT do

Annual cost is annual — it doesn't model when the cost hits. Summer is heavier (more hours per person) than winter, so a raise effective in May costs more than the same raise effective in November. The annual number is the right number for budgeting; cash-flow timing is a separate conversation.
