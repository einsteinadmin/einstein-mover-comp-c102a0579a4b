📖 How to use this tool

What this is

A live scenario modeler for mover pay. Plug in different combinations of base pay and variable pay per position, and see the company-wide cost, the gross-margin impact, and what the announcement looks like to an individual mover. The point isn't to predict the future — it's to make trade-offs visible before we commit to anything.

Three scenarios, one baseline. Everything is compared against Current (locked, not editable). Scenarios A, B, and C are sandboxes you can edit. Switch between them with the tabs above the position matrix.

The three pre-loaded scenarios

Scenario A — "Flat $20 Co-Pilot, raises across leads." Co-Pilot base jumps to $20/hr but loses its variable component (Cameron's hypothesis: simpler entry-level pay attracts more applicants and removes a confusing piece of the offer). All lead positions get base + variable raises. Net impact ≈ +$558K / +6% vs current.
Scenario B — "$20 Co-Pilot keeping the variable." Same as A, but Co-Pilots also keep their $1.50/hr variable. Tests Paul's hypothesis that variable still matters at entry-level. Lead raises identical to A. Net impact ≈ +$850K / +9% vs current.
Scenario C — "No-change baseline." A copy of Current, editable. Useful when you want to test ONE specific change in isolation without rebuilding from scratch.

The position matrix — what each column means

  • Headcount — total people in that position company-wide.
  • Diff HC — how many of that headcount sit at Houston + San Antonio (see "Branch differential" below).
  • Hrs/yr — blended average hours per year per person at that position. Should reflect your real FT/PT mix across the full year (summer is heavier, winter lighter — average it).
  • Base $/hr — what standard branches pay as base. Houston + SAA pay this minus the differential.
  • Var Max $/hr — the maximum quarterly variable a high performer can earn (same at every branch).
  • TCC Low / Med / High — Total Cash Comp per hour at each performance tier (base + earned variable).
  • Raise % L / M / H — how much each tier moves vs Current at the same performance level. Apples-to-apples.

Branch differential (Houston + San Antonio)

Houston and San Antonio pay base $1/hr below the other 8 branches across every position. The variable component is identical company-wide. The "Branch Differential $/hr" global lets you change that delta — set it to 0 to model a unified pay band, or test -0.50 to see what closing half the gap costs.

The Diff HC column tells the math how many of each position's headcount are at the differential branches. Defaults seed at 20% of each position (rough estimate — 2 of 10 branches). The "Apply % to all positions" button lets you bulk-fill all rows at once if you want a different starting estimate.

Why this matters: a $1/hr base raise that "feels" cheap costs roughly $90K/year just for the ~50 movers at Houston + SAA, on top of the standard-branch cost. Modeling the two cohorts separately keeps the total honest.

Performance distribution & multipliers

The annual cost calc assumes most movers earn the middle tier of variable, not the max. You tell the model two things:

  • Distribution — what % of movers we expect to be Low / Medium / High performers. Default 20 / 60 / 20.
  • Variable multipliers — what % of the max variable each tier actually earns. Default 50% / 80% / 100%.

So a position with $2.00/hr max variable, under defaults, costs roughly 0.2×$1.00 + 0.6×$1.60 + 0.2×$2.00 = $1.56/hr in expected variable on average. Adjust to match what your actuals show.

FTE basis & Implied FTEs

Raw headcount doesn't tell you what the workload actually is — a part-time mover working 800 hrs/yr counts as "1 head" but is really 0.4 of a full-time-equivalent (at a 2,080 hr basis).

The "Implied FTEs" card divides total mover-hours by the FTE basis (default 2,080 = 40 hrs × 52 wks). If the number is meaningfully below raw headcount, you've got a heavy PT mix and the per-position Hrs/yr inputs should reflect that blended average.

This does not affect cost math. Cost is driven by Headcount × Hrs/yr × $/hr. The Implied FTE card is a sanity-check so you can spot when Hrs/yr defaults are too high (or too low) for a position.

Mover-view preview

The bottom section shows what an individual mover at a given position sees when we announce the new program. Pick the position, scenario, and branch tier (Standard vs. Houston/SAA). The annual TCC row is calibrated to that position's Hrs/yr — so it's a realistic year-one earnings projection at low / medium / high performance.

Use this to sanity-check that the announcement actually feels like a raise to the mover you're talking to. If the high-performer line moves but the low-performer line is flat, that's worth knowing before you ship.

Aggregate impact — three affordability lenses

The Aggregate Impact section is split into three modules, each with its own ceiling and its own audience:

  • 🚚 Mover Affordability — total mover wages, true loaded mover cost (wages × burden multiplier), Mover Cost ÷ Revenue (loaded; vs the 40% ceiling), unloaded wages ÷ revenue (reference), blended $/hr, base/variable split, headcount, implied FTEs. The 40% ceiling tone applies to the LOADED card.
  • 👔 Manager + Leadership Affordability — Manager Comp ÷ AGM (vs the 16% ceiling per the philosophy doc), plus Manager Comp ÷ Revenue as a secondary view. Only populates if you fill in "Manager + Leadership Comp $" in globals (otherwise grayed out).
  • 📊 Combined / All-In — placeholder/working set of revenue-based ratios: Total Comp ÷ Revenue, Total Comp ÷ AGM, Revenue per Mover Head, Revenue per FTE. We'll refine which metrics matter most once we've used it a few times.

Why three lenses: mover and manager comp scale differently and answer different questions. Mover comp is variable-with-revenue (driven by headcount × hours × wage). Manager comp is fixed (salaried). Keeping them separate lets each ceiling do its job without the math getting tangled.

Sharing & saving

Use the 📎 Copy share link button (bottom of page). It packs your full state — all 3 scenarios, every input — into the URL and copies it to your clipboard. Paste in Slack or email and the recipient sees exactly what you see. State also auto-saves to your browser; close the tab and your work comes back.

One thing this tool does NOT do

Annual cost is annual — it doesn't model when the cost hits. Summer is heavier (more hours per person) than winter, so a raise effective in May costs more than the same raise effective in November. The annual number is the right number for budgeting; cash-flow timing is a separate conversation.

4. Architecture (the whole tool in 200 words)

Single file. ~2,000 lines of HTML/CSS/JS. No build. No dependencies. No backend.

State model. One JavaScript object state holds everything: globals (revenue, ceilings, distributions, multipliers, branch differential, FTE basis), positions (7 positions × {headcount, diffHc, hrsPerYr, base{current,A,B,C}, varMax{current,A,B,C}}), plus UI state (activeTab, mover view selections, hiddenCols).

Persistence. localStorage key einstein-mover-comp-modeler-v1. State also URL-encodable via "📎 Copy share link" (base64 JSON in URL hash).

Migration. Forward-compatible migrateState() fills in new fields from defaults when older saved/shared state loads. Always add a migration block when introducing new globals or position fields.

Render loop. renderAll() → renderGlobals() → renderTabs() → renderMatrix() → renderAggregate() → renderMoverView(). Re-runs on every input change. Cheap enough to not need optimization.

Calc. Two pure functions: calcPosition(posId, scenario) returns per-position economics. calcScenarioTotals(scenario) rolls up to company totals. No state mutation — easy to reason about.

6. Branch differential math

For each position, headcount splits into two cohorts:

  • Standard branches (8 of 10): headcount − diffHc people earn base
  • Houston + SAA: diffHc people earn base + branchDiff

Variable is identical across all branches. Annual cost:

annualBase = (HC − DiffHC) × hrs × base + DiffHC × hrs × (base + branchDiff)
annualVar = HC × hrs × expectedVarPerHr
expectedVarPerHr = varMax × (distLow×multLow + distMed×multMed + distHigh×multHigh) / 10000
annualCost = annualBase + annualVar

9. Common edits — HOW TO

Update headcount / Diff HC defaults: edit defaultState(), in positions: block, change headcount / diffHc. Save, copy to deploy, push.

Change a default Base/Var Max for Current: edit base.current or varMax.current in defaultState(). Reconsider whether A/B/C defaults still represent intended hypothesis.

Add a new column to the matrix:

  1. Add <th> to the <thead> row. Include class="col-<name>" if hideable
  2. In renderMatrix()'s tr.innerHTML template, add a <td> in the right position
  3. If hideable: add col-<name> to the <td>, add to HIDEABLE_COLS constant, add checkbox in Columns popover, add CSS rule table.matrix.hide-col-<name> .col-<name> { display: none; }
  4. If it affects totals: update <tfoot> cell count + group-divider colspan (currently 19)
  5. Add to CSV export header + per-row values

Add a new affordability metric: Module 3 (Combined / All-In) is the placeholder home. In renderAggregate(), find // ---------- Module 3 block, add a <div class="stat placeholder"> card using existing cmp/cur totals. If you need new data, extend calcScenarioTotals().

Tweak a ceiling default: change in defaultState(), also update <input> value="..." in HTML so fresh loads match. Update migration if old saved state should remap.

Add a new global: add to defaultState().globals, add to globalFields array, add migration shim filling default for older state, add <input> in HTML, wire to renderGlobals().

10. Calculation reference (plain language)

TCC at Low/Med/High = base + varMax × (mult / 100)
Expected variable/hr = varMax × (dist × mult sums) / 10000
Annual position cost = (HC − DiffHC) × hrs × base + DiffHC × hrs × (base + branchDiff) + HC × hrs × expectedVar/hr

Total Mover Comp = Σ annualCost across positions
Mover Comp ÷ Revenue = Total Mover Comp / Revenue
Mover Comp ÷ AGM = Total Mover Comp / (Revenue × AGM%)
Implied FTEs = Total Mover-Hours / FTE basis

Manager Comp ÷ AGM = otherComp / (Revenue × AGM%)
Manager Comp ÷ Revenue = otherComp / Revenue

Total All-In Comp = Total Mover Comp + otherComp
Total Comp ÷ Revenue = Total All-In Comp / Revenue
Revenue per Head = Revenue / Total Headcount
Revenue per FTE = Revenue / Implied FTEs

Raise % at level X = (Scenario TCC at X − Current TCC at X) / Current TCC at X × 100

11. Known limitations & open questions

  • Annual ≠ phased. Tool models annual cost only. Summer is heavier than winter — a May-effective raise costs more than a November-effective one. Use the annual number for budgeting; phasing is a separate conversation.
  • Diff HC defaults are guesses (20%/position). Replace with payroll actuals when available.
  • Performance distribution defaults are guesses (20/60/20). Plug in real ratings data when Anne's 100-point system produces it.
  • Manager Comp ÷ AGM cohort definition is still unsettled. Philosophy doc cites ~16% ceiling but doesn't fully specify the cohort. Ben quoted 13.4% YoY on 5/11. Confirm cohort with Ben before treating ceiling as a hard go/no-go.
  • Variable comp assumes multiplier model — fixed % of max per tier. If we shift to continuous scorecard-driven variable, the dist + mult model gets replaced.
  • Combined / All-In is intentionally placeholder. Five metrics are working but the right set is TBD.
  • No undo inside the tool. Resetting all scenarios wipes prior state. Use share links to snapshot, or "Copy JSON" before reset.
  • State migration is robust as of v2.7.2. Missing globals, missing positions, and missing UI-state fields all fall back to defaults. Truly corrupted JSON in localStorage still requires a hard reset.
